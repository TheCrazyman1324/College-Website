Why Choose Engineering

Job Satisfaction

It’s ideal to find a career that you enjoy. After all, you’ll likely spend eight hours or more a day, five days a week, at your job. Engineering can provide a satisfying field of work.

Variety of Career Opportunities

From electrical and civil engineering to mechanical and biomedical engineering, an engineering degree offers many career possibilities. The majority of today’s college graduates will have more than one career during their work life, and engineering can provide a strong foundation for almost any one of them.

Potential to Benefit Society

As an engineer, you can choose to work on projects that benefit society. Engineers help clean the environment, develop prosthetic aids, create clean and efficient transportation systems, find new sources of energy, alleviate the world’s hunger problems, and increase the standard of living in underdeveloped countries.

Challenging Work

There is no shortage of solutions to be discovered in engineering. There is no single answer, no answer in the back of the book, no professor to tell you that you are right or wrong. You must devise a solution and persuade others that your solution is the best one.

Intellectual Development

An engineering education will “exercise” your brain, developing your ability to think logically and solve problems. These skills will be valuable throughout your life – and not only when you are solving engineering problems.

Creative Thinking

Engineering is a creative profession. Because we are in a time of rapid social and technological changes, the need for engineers to think creatively is greater now than ever before. If you like to question, explore, invent, discover, and create, then engineering could be the ideal profession for you.

Technological and Scientific Discovery

Do you know why golf balls have dimples on them? Or why split-level houses experience more damage in earthquakes? Engineering education can help you answer questions and push you to ask new questions and find new solutions.

Financial Security

Engineering is a lucrative career with an increasing growth outlook in years to come. Engineering graduates receive the highest starting salary of any discipline.

Prestige

Engineers help sustain our nation's international competitiveness, maintain our standard of living, ensure national security, and protect public safety. As a member of such a respected profession, you will receive a high amount of prestige.

Professional Environment

As an engineer, you will work in a professional environment and have a certain amount of freedom in choosing your work. Engineers have the opportunity to learn and grow through both on-the-job and formal training.
  • Popular specializations

    • Computer Science Engineering

      Computer Science Engineering (CSE) is a comprehensive academic program that combines the domains of Computer Engineering and Computer Science. It stands as a highly desirable course among engineering students due to its immense popularity. CSE holds immense significance in today's technological landscape and offers promising career opportunities for aspiring engineers.

    • Electrical Engineering

      Electrical Engineering is a versatile specialization that focuses on the study and application of electricity, electronics, and electromagnetism. It deals with the design, development, and maintenance of electrical systems, including power generation, transmission, and distribution. Electrical Engineering plays a vital role in various industries such as power systems, electronics, telecommunications, and renewable energy.

    • Mechanical Engineering

      Mechanical Engineering involves the design, analysis, and manufacturing of mechanical systems. It encompasses various areas such as thermodynamics, fluid mechanics, materials science, and mechanical design. Mechanical engineers contribute to industries like automotive, aerospace, energy, robotics, and manufacturing. They play a crucial role in creating innovative solutions to complex engineering challenges.

    • Electronics and Communication Engineering

      Electronics and Communication Engineering (ECE) deals with the design, development, and maintenance of electronic devices, communication systems, and information processing. It encompasses areas like analog and digital electronics, signal processing, wireless communication, embedded systems, and microelectronics. ECE graduates find opportunities in industries such as telecommunications, semiconductor technology, consumer electronics, and research and development.

    • Civil Engineering

      Civil Engineering focuses on the design, construction, and maintenance of physical infrastructure, including buildings, bridges, roads, dams, and water supply systems. It involves areas such as structural engineering, geotechnical engineering, transportation engineering, and environmental engineering. Civil engineers contribute to the development of society by creating sustainable and resilient infrastructure.

    • Marine Engineering

      Marine Engineering involves the design, construction, operation, and maintenance of ships, boats, offshore structures, and related equipment. It covers areas such as naval architecture, marine propulsion systems, marine materials, and marine systems integration. Marine engineers contribute to the maritime industry and are involved in shipbuilding, ship design, marine equipment manufacturing, and offshore exploration and production.

    • Aerospace Engineering

      Aerospace Engineering focuses on the design, development, and testing of aircraft, spacecraft, and related technologies. It encompasses areas such as aerodynamics, propulsion systems, materials science, and flight mechanics. Aerospace engineers work in the aviation and aerospace industries, contributing to the advancement of air and space travel.

    • Biomedical Engineering

      Biomedical Engineering combines principles of engineering and biology to develop solutions in the healthcare field. It involves areas such as medical device design, biomaterials, tissue engineering, and medical imaging. Biomedical engineers contribute to the development of innovative technologies and advancements in medical diagnostics, treatment, and patient care.

    • Chemical Engineering

      Chemical Engineering involves the application of chemistry, physics, and engineering principles to design and operate chemical processes. It includes areas such as process design, chemical reaction engineering, materials synthesis, and process control. Chemical engineers work in industries such as pharmaceuticals, petrochemicals, food processing, energy, and environmental engineering.
